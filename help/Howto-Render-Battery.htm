This is the Battery Renderer

The Battery Renderer is the main feature in the Rom Fumbler. There are lots of styles to select from in the dropdown box above.
The Renderer creates your battery-icons with graphical and mathmatical algorithms. There are no prestored battery icons!
The Icons are created/calculated online, when you press the play-button.

On the left hand side you find all the battery settings you can play with. So please play with the settings and check out what happens :-)

Saving and restoring Battery Settings

Every battery style has its own set of Battery Settings! ...and its own predefinitions within the Battery Settings when you start the Rom Fumbler.
After you made your modifications, you can save your settings and later restore them. You can even restore them to another style! (This may not always be usefull :-) )

Settings are not stored automaticly on program exit!
...so if you tweaked a lot of stuff, than you should save the battery settings and load them when you start the Rom Fumbler again!

You can save and load these settings with the two buttons on the top-left.

Rom Settings

Rom Settings also contribute to your battery icons. In Rom Settings you set

  • the icon height
  • naming pattern of the icons
  • resizing options (smart resize)
  • the drawable folder used in the flashable-zip

Charge Symbol

The dropdownbox with charge icons has some icons that are compiled into the Java jar.
But you can add your own icons as well.
Look at the subfolder:

  • ./custom/charge/...

and put your own additional charge icons in there and restart the Rom Fumbler. The icons must be transparent png's! (No gif, no jpg...!)

Overview & Listview

The overview shows all rendered icons in a grid. This overview is stored as a png to disc every time you press the play-button!
This overvies and the battery png's are stored in a subfolder of the Rom Fumbler:

  • ./pngs/renderer/batt/<battery-style-name>/...

So if you want the png's only and no flashable zip...then look in that subfolder(s)!

Animator

At the bottom you find "the Animator" with the "Play" and "Stop" button. Press "Play" to see how the icon you created animates, when played in sequence!
